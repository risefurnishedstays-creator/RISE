Accessibility Statement

We want booking a stay to be easy for everyone, including guests who use assistive technology. Here’s how we approach accessibility — and how to tell us when we fall short.

⟳ Last updated June 2026
On this page
  1. Our commitment
  2. What we do
  3. Standards we aim for
  4. Known limitations
  5. Tell us how we’re doing

01 Our commitment

RISE Furnished Stays is committed to making this booking website usable for as many people as possible, regardless of ability or the technology they use. Accessibility is part of how we build and maintain the site, not an afterthought.

02 What we do

  • Use clear, semantic structure so screen readers can navigate headings, links, and lists.
  • Maintain strong color contrast between text and backgrounds.
  • Support full keyboard navigation through menus, the booking flow, and forms.
  • Write descriptive labels and alternative text for images and controls.
  • Design layouts that adapt to phones, tablets, and zoomed-in screens.

03 Standards we aim for

We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible to people with a wide range of disabilities, and we use them as our benchmark when building and reviewing the site.

04 Known limitations

Some parts of the site rely on third-party tools — such as the availability calendar, embedded maps, and payment processing — whose accessibility we don’t fully control. If any of these create a barrier for you, contact us and we’ll help you complete your booking another way.

05 Tell us how we’re doing

If you run into a barrier on this site, or need information or booking help in a different format, we want to hear from you.
